# SIC Coach Lite — Soccer Scope v1

## Status
Draft v1

## Purpose

This document defines the sport scope for SIC Coach Lite v1.

SIC as a platform may support multiple sports over time, but the first release of Coach Lite is intentionally focused on soccer.

This document defines what is in scope, what is out of scope, and how soccer support should be interpreted in the first version.

---

## Scope Decision

Coach Lite v1 is soccer-first.

That means:
- the product experience is designed for soccer
- the first content library is soccer-specific
- the first diagrams are soccer-specific
- the first validation rules are soccer-specific
- the first methodology use cases are soccer club use cases

This does not prevent future multi-sport support.
It simply defines the launch scope.

---

## Why Soccer First

Soccer is the right first sport because it offers:
- strong founder-context alignment
- common grassroots and club use cases
- repeatable training structures
- reusable visual diagram patterns
- a clean path to futsal later

A focused first sport is more valuable than a weak multi-sport launch.

---

## In-Scope Contexts

### Team environments
- grassroots youth teams
- recreational youth teams
- competitive youth club teams
- academy-style youth groups
- camps and clinics
- solo coach or small staff environments

### Session environments
- outdoor grass fields
- outdoor turf fields
- indoor spaces large enough for soccer activity
- small-sided areas
- half-field and reduced-field training environments

### Age bands
- U8 to U10
- U11 to U14
- U15 to U19
- adult amateur
- general recreational

---

## In-Scope Session Categories

Coach Lite v1 should support:

- warm up
- passing and receiving
- first touch
- dribbling
- ball mastery
- possession
- defending
- pressing
- finishing
- transition
- small-sided games
- technical activation
- game-like practices

---

## In-Scope Inputs

The system should support input such as:

- age group
- level
- number of players
- number of coaches
- session duration
- field size or field type
- cones
- balls
- bibs or pinnies
- goals or mini goals
- poles or mannequins if available
- focus area
- weather or space limitation
- desired intensity

---

## In-Scope Output Behavior

For soccer v1, the system should produce:

- a full session plan
- activity sequence
- minute totals
- setup instructions
- coaching points
- progressions and regressions
- clear soccer drill diagrams
- exportable session pack

The output should be understandable by a real coach preparing a real training session.

---

## Diagram Expectations for Soccer v1

Soccer diagrams in v1 should support:
- field or area boundaries
- cones
- balls
- player markers
- mannequins or poles
- goals or mini goals
- pass arrows
- dribble arrows
- movement arrows
- rotation cues
- pressing direction indicators
- labels or simple role markers

The diagrams do not need to look artistic.
They must look clear, repeatable, and useful.

---

## Out of Scope for v1

The following are out of scope for the first release:

- basketball
- volleyball
- swimming
- baseball
- American football
- rugby
- advanced goalkeeper-specialist session support
- fully editable diagram board tools
- full match analysis
- video-based drill extraction
- performance science personalization
- medical or rehab planning
- long-term periodization engine

---

## Borderline Cases

### Futsal
Not in v1 launch scope, but should be treated as the first likely expansion after soccer.

### Goalkeeper sessions
Basic goalkeeper inclusion inside general team activities is acceptable.
Dedicated goalkeeper-only planning is not a primary v1 target.

### Indoor soccer adaptation
Allowed if it is presented as a constrained soccer variation rather than a separate sport product.

---

## Quality Bar for Soccer v1

A soccer session should only be considered valid if:

- the duration totals are correct
- the activities fit the available equipment
- the session is age-appropriate
- the diagrams match the setup described in text
- the coach can understand how to run the activity without guessing
- the session can still be understood if the diagram is unavailable

---

## Product Messaging Guidance

The right message is:

SIC Coach Lite is built specifically for soccer in its first release.

Avoid saying:
- all sports supported
- works for every coaching environment
- advanced sports science engine
- fully customizable whiteboard replacement

The early product should feel focused and trustworthy.

---

## Summary

Soccer scope v1 is intentionally narrow.

It gives SIC Coach Lite a clear launch identity, improves output quality, and creates a stronger path for future expansion into futsal and eventually additional sports.
